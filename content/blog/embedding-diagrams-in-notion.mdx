## Why embed instead of screenshot

Screenshots of diagrams go stale the moment something changes. With a Glyphic embed, your Notion doc always shows the current version of the diagram — no manual updates needed.

## Getting the embed URL

1. Open the diagram you want to embed in Glyphic.
2. Click **Share** in the top-right corner.
3. Toggle **Public link** on.
4. Copy the **Embed URL** (it looks like `https://glyphic.cc/embed/d/abc123`).

## Adding the embed to Notion

1. In Notion, type `/embed` and select **Embed**.
2. Paste the Glyphic embed URL.
3. Click **Embed link**.

Notion will render the diagram inline. It's interactive — viewers can pan and zoom without leaving Notion.

## Controlling access

By default, anyone with the Notion page link can view the embedded diagram. If your Notion workspace is internal-only, this is fine — the embed respects the Notion page permissions.

If you need tighter control:

- Toggle **Public link** off to make the embed private. Only logged-in Glyphic users with workspace access will be able to view it.
- Use **Share → Invite** to grant view access to specific people.

## Keeping diagrams up to date

Edits you make in Glyphic appear in the Notion embed immediately — there's no re-embed step. If a viewer has the Notion page open, they'll see updates on the next page refresh.
